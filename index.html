Orbital Dynamicist, Data Scientist, Machine Learning Engineer

I received my PhD at the University of MD, College Park in 2020 while studying Resonances in Ring, Satellite, and Planetary Systems . Since then, I have worked as a postdoc at NASA GSFC on the Juno Magnetometer Science team and, as of April 2022, as a machine learning engineer at United States Pharmacoepia building a model to predict drug shortages.

My expertise includes all matters orbital mechanics, along with using random forests to model imbalanced datasets.
